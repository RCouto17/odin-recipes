Potato Rosti

Description

The third best food in the world, hands down

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Salt
  • Finely ground black pepper
  • Clarified butter

Steps

  1. Peel then grate the potatoes using a box grater, trying to get nice long strands if you can.

  2. Grab handfuls of potato and squeeze out excess liquid, then place in bowl. This helps make the potato crispier (otherwise all that water has to steam out in the pan).

  3. Add butter, salt and pepper then toss. Don't worry if your potatoes go brown/reddy - it's still safe to eat, and turns white again when cooked.

  4. Melt half the clarified butter in a 26cm / 10.5" (or thereabouts) non-stick pan or skillet over medium low heat. Shallow non-stick pan with sloped sides is best for easy rosti removal.

  5. Place the potato in pan, but do not pack down. Use rubber spatula to tidy the edges, then lightly pat down to even surface.

  6. Cook first side: Cook 12 minutes until underside is very golden and crispy, lifting edge with rubber spatula to check. If by 8 minutes it is not going golden, increase heat.

  7. Flip: Cover pan with a round wooden board with a handle (or anything similar with a handle, Note 3). Holding the handle of the board, flip quickly - and with confidence!

  8. Cook second side: Melt remaining clarified butter in the pan, then slide the rosti back in and cook for 12 minutes until golden crispy, and there is no resistance when a knife is inserted into the middle.
